<p><b style="font-family: inherit; font-size: inherit; text-align: inherit; background-color: initial;">Control engineering</b> or <b style="font-family: inherit; font-size: inherit; text-align: inherit; background-color: initial;">control systems engineering</b> is the <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Engineering" style="font-family: inherit; font-size: inherit; font-weight: inherit; text-align: inherit;">engineering</a> discipline that applies <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Control_theory" style="font-family: inherit; font-size: inherit; font-weight: inherit; text-align: inherit;">control theory</a> to design systems with desired behaviors. The practice uses <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sensors" style="font-family: inherit; font-size: inherit; font-weight: inherit; text-align: inherit;">sensors</a> to measure the output performance of the device being controlled and those measurements can be used to give <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Feedback" style="font-family: inherit; font-size: inherit; font-weight: inherit; text-align: inherit;">feedback</a> to the input <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Actuators" style="font-family: inherit; font-size: inherit; font-weight: inherit; text-align: inherit;">actuators</a> that can make corrections toward desired performance. When a device is designed to perform without the need of human inputs for correction it is called <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Automatic_control" style="font-family: inherit; font-size: inherit; font-weight: inherit; text-align: inherit;">automatic control</a> (such as <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cruise_control" style="font-family: inherit; font-size: inherit; font-weight: inherit; text-align: inherit;">cruise control</a> for regulating a car's speed). <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Multi-disciplinary" style="font-family: inherit; font-size: inherit; font-weight: inherit; text-align: inherit;">Multi-disciplinary</a> in nature, control systems engineering activities focus on implementation of control systems mainly derived by <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mathematical_model" style="font-family: inherit; font-size: inherit; font-weight: inherit; text-align: inherit;">mathematical modeling</a> of <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/System" style="font-family: inherit; font-size: inherit; font-weight: inherit; text-align: inherit;">systems</a> of a diverse range.</p>
